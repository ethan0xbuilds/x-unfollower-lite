# Privacy Policy — X Unfollower Lite

**Last updated:** 2026-07-16

X Unfollower Lite (“the Extension”) is designed to process data **only on your device**.

## What the Extension accesses

- Pages on `https://x.com/*` and `https://twitter.com/*` so it can read the **following list UI** you already see in the browser and simulate button clicks you could perform yourself.
- It may observe **responses already returned to your browser tab** by X (for example GraphQL payloads used to render the following list) in order to read public profile metrics such as follower counts. This stays inside your browser session and is not uploaded by the Extension.
- It does **not** ask you for your X password.
- It does **not** call a third-party backend operated by us.

## Data stored locally

Using Chrome’s `chrome.storage.local`, the Extension may store:

- Your preferences (daily limit, delay range, mutual protection toggle, follower filters)
- A whitelist of handles you choose to protect
- A **daily unfollow counter** (date + count) for rate limiting

This data stays in your browser profile unless you clear extension data or uninstall.

## Data we do not collect

The Extension authors do **not** operate servers that receive:

- Your X account credentials
- Your following list
- Unfollow history
- Analytics, advertising IDs, or crash phone-home data

If you obtained the Extension from the Chrome Web Store, Google may process standard install/usage metrics under Google’s policies; that is independent of this Extension’s code.

## Third parties

The Extension interacts with X’s website in your browser session. Your use of X remains governed by X’s terms and privacy policy. The Extension authors are not responsible for X’s data practices.

## Children

The Extension is not directed at children under 13.

## Changes

We may update this policy when features change. The “Last updated” date at the top will change accordingly. Significant changes will be reflected in the store listing or repository.

## Contact

For privacy questions, open an issue on the public GitHub repository for this project, or contact the publisher email listed on the Chrome Web Store listing.
